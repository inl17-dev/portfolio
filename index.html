Field of Study

I’m an Environmental Science student at the University of Pittsburgh pursuing a certificate in GIS, with interests in remote sensing and spatial data analysis. My academic work and projects have explored how spatial tools can be used to understand environmental change, including topics such as invasive species expansion, water quality, and urban sustainability.

I have experience with ArcGIS Pro, ENVI, and R, and I enjoy translating complex environmental data into clear insights. I’m particularly interested in applying geospatial techniques to support environmental management, climate resilience, and conservation planning.

Experience and Projects

Phragmites Australis Subsp. Australis & Climate Change​

GEOL1490 - GIS Independent Study Capstone

This independent research project investigates the relationship between climate change indicators and the spatial expansion of Phragmites australis in the Delaware Bay region from 2013 to 2023. Using GIS and climate data analysis, the goal is to assess how environmental changes may be facilitating this invasive species' growth.​

Exploring the Relationship Between Mineral Content and Temperature in Saline Valley

GEOL1460 - Introduction to Remote Sensing

This independent research project investigates the relationship between ground mineral content and temperature in Saline Valley. Using the provided datasets and remote sensing techniques, the goal is to understand the spatial and temporal patterns of these variables and their interconnections.

Interests

I’m interested in environmental sustainability, conservation, and climate resilience, particularly how geospatial tools can support better decision-making. I also enjoy hands-on environmental work and volunteering in local efforts, where I can directly contribute to restoration and learn more about ecological systems.

Outside of that, I enjoy spending time in nature, especially exploring parks and green spaces. I like trying new food, drawing digital art, and collecting rocks, minerals, and small trinkets. In my free time, I also enjoy playing video games and watching shows, and I’m interested in getting more into birdwatching, hiking, and improving my plant and wildlife identification skills.

Diversity Statement

I value creating inclusive and accessible spaces within environmental science, a field where access to resources and opportunities is not always equitable. Through my volunteer work in local environmental efforts, I have seen how community-based initiatives can connect people to their surrounding ecosystems while also addressing environmental challenges. These experiences have shaped my understanding of the importance of making environmental work more inclusive and community-centered.

As I continue my academic and professional development, I aim to contribute to environments that welcome diverse perspectives and experiences. I am particularly interested in using geospatial tools to support communities in understanding and addressing environmental issues that directly impact them. By combining technical skills with a commitment to inclusivity, I hope to help make environmental science more accessible and responsive to a wider range of communities.
